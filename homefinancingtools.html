Understanding Your Mortgage

A mortgage is a loan taken out to buy property or land. The loan is secured against the value of your home until it's paid off. It's crucial to understand how much you can afford to borrow before committing to a mortgage.

Types of Mortgages

There are many types of mortgages available, including fixed-rate, adjustable-rate, FHA, VA, and jumbo loans. Each has its own advantages depending on your financial situation and home buying goals.

Debt-to-Income (DTI) Calculator

Your debt-to-income ratio (DTI) is a key factor lenders use to determine how much you can borrow. A lower DTI ratio means you have less debt compared to your income.

What is DTI?

Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio is a personal finance measure that compares your monthly debt payments to your monthly gross income. Lenders use this ratio to determine your borrowing risk and how much you can afford to borrow.

Why DTI Matters

Most lenders prefer a DTI ratio of 36% or less, with no more than 28% of that debt going toward servicing your mortgage. A lower DTI ratio demonstrates your ability to manage monthly payments and repay debts responsibly.

Improving Your DTI

To improve your DTI ratio, focus on paying down existing debt, increasing your income, or both. Consider creating a debt repayment plan and avoid taking on new debt before applying for a mortgage.
